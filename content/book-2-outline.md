# BOOK 2: THE SEARCH

**POV:** Flint (primary), ensemble
**Era:** Same as Book 1

---

## Sequence of Events

1. **Aftermath.** Iselle is gone. She walked through a window and vanished. Flint, Cam, Liv, and Milton are left with one question: where did she go, and how do they follow? Flint is devastated but refuses to accept that Iselle is simply gone. The quest is singular and urgent: find Iselle.

2. **The search begins.** The group throws itself into finding Iselle. Window experimentation with new urgency: new locations, new emotional triggers, new approaches. Everything fails. Dead ends. Windows that open to the wrong places. Windows that don't open at all. They know windows are real, they know Iselle went through one, but they have no map, no rules, and no guarantee they can reach wherever she is.

3. **Theo and Mira.** Flint's friends from his ordinary world step in. They don't fully understand what's happening (windows, ArcSec, the supernatural elements) but they care about Flint and can see he's in crisis. Theo actively joins the search. Mira may help as well. How much they learn about the true nature of what's happening, and how they process it, is part of this book's character work. (Their specific roles and arcs need development.)

4. **The group under pressure.** The found family is tested as a working unit for the first time. In Book 1, Cam and Liv were the DJ couple, Milton was the quiet presence, and the group bonded through shared wonder and loyalty. Now they're bonded through desperation and mission. Who Cam is when the music stops and the stakes are life-or-death. Who Liv is when she's not performing but planning. Who Milton really is beneath the silence. And Theo and Mira, ordinary people in an extraordinary situation, finding their place in a group that operates on a level they barely comprehend.

5. **The heist plan takes shape.** Through the search, the group identifies the right window: a control window in a club in Berlin, guarded, in a VIP room with a gold frame and deep red curtains. Flint has to go through and close it behind him. The plan: Cam handles the power tap and pyrotechnics, Olivia handles the board and gets the keypad door open, Flint gets smuggled in inside a road case with the equipment.

6. **The club heist.** The van arrives at the loading dock. Cam argues with venue security about Milton's laminated badge. They load in. Flint counts time inside the road case. When he climbs out behind the stage, Olivia signals from the FOH desk: two fingers up, point right. He gets into position by the speaker stack at the corridor entrance. Two guards come out of the keypad door and stop at the junction. Cam hits the power tap. Every overhead blows at once, white sparks rain over the stage. The crowd screams, then decides it's the best part of the night. Backup emergency reds kick in. The fog rig opens. The corridor guards turn toward the stage. Flint goes past them low and fast, finds the keypad door already ajar, and gets through to the VIP room.

7. **The VIP room.** Two guards flank the window, gold frame, deep red curtains. Both guards clock Flint. Milton appears on the nearer guard's shoulder out of nowhere, eleven pounds of cat, claws in, staring at the guy's face from four inches. The guard grabs for the cat, and that's one guard dealt with. The other one is bigger than Flint by a full head. He gets hold of Flint, walks him into the wall, pins him with a forearm across the chest. Flint fights but can't get leverage, the guy has sixty pounds on him. Flint is starting to lose consciousness. Iselle appears behind the guard and destroys him from behind. She grabs Flint's sequin jacket and tries to move him as hard as she can: YOU HAVE TO GO NOW. Flint is delirious, full of everything he's feeling, but he does what she says.

8. **Through the window.** Flint goes through arms first, turning in the air. Through the window behind him he sees the room, red emergency light, smoke drifting in. He sees the first guard with his arm out. He sees Iselle at the window, facing him, pulling it down. A flash, white, from behind her. Something that isn't light comes out of her mouth. The window closes and disappears. Blackout.

**Book 2 ends.** From the real world's perspective: Iselle is dead. Flint is gone, fell through a window and never came back. Cam, Liv, and Milton are left in the aftermath.
