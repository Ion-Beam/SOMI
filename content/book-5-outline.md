# BOOK 5: THE FINAL FORGING

**POV:** Ensemble (Flint, Iselle, Cam, Liv) + Cole's background (~first 25%)
**Era:** Same as Book 4

---

## Sequence of Events

### Part I: Cole's Background (~25% of book)

1. **The twins.** The prophecy said one son. There were two. Their mother, a Nacreid, chose Flint to fulfill the prophecy. Cole was embarrassed by Flint's behavior and angry that his mother let him get away with it.

2. **The extinction event.** The mother designed the prophecy to trigger an imbalance that would cause the Nacreids to win. Instead, Flint and Iselle's union backfired. The Nacreids were banished from earth, forced underground. The Irisyne left to become the stars. Humans were created from the mixing. The mother chose the wrong twin.

3. **The mother's confession.** After the annihilation, Cole's mother tells him the truth. She chose wrong. Cole was the perfect one, the intended pairing with Iselle, the one who should have been sent to destroy the Irisyne. The annihilation failed because she gambled on the wrong son.

4. **The forgotten rule.** Cole's mother reveals what she stole from the Irisyne King she loved: if one race kills the other, both annihilate. She designed the prophecy to trigger an imbalance that would cause the Nacreids to win. Instead they were banished from earth.

5. **The curse.** The imperfect annihilation created a reincarnation cycle. Flint and Iselle bound to find each other, fall in love, and die without experiencing their love. Cole bound to reincarnate as well, his soul tied to Flint's due to the prophecy. Unbreakable.

6. **Cole across lifetimes.** Centuries of watching Flint be chosen, by Iselle, by the cosmic system, by everyone. Cole accumulates across lives. Nacreid continuity means he carries everything forward. Every life, the same wound.

7. **Cole traps his mother.** Cole finds a way to imprison her: a vessel, artifact, or mechanism within ArcSec infrastructure. She is alive but contained.

8. **Cole's promise.** He tells his mother he will break the curse AND eliminate both races outright. Not her failed version. His engineered version. He will finish what she started.

9. **The infiltration of ArcSec.** Cole's centuries-long project. He corrupted their records across multiple lifetimes, ensuring he's pulled back in every time he's reborn. He rose through the ranks from the inside.

10. **The carry-out.** During the island fire in Book 1, Cole carried Flint out of the flames after winning the fight. Revealed here for the first time.

11. **Cole's command of ArcSec in the present.** The institution meant to prevent the end of everything now has the end of everything in its boardroom. Technology, personnel, institutional knowledge, the gate between realms, every piece serves his plan.

### Part II: The Gang Tries to Stop It

12. **Oil in the water.** The water realm is corrupted. Still water turns to oil. The surface becomes turbulent as the gate begins to open.

13. **The gang knows.** Flint, Iselle, Cam, Liv, and Milton understand what Cole is planning: the oil gate, the annihilation, the destruction of both races. They have Cam and Liv's years of ArcSec intelligence, Iselle's centuries of documented knowledge, Flint's understanding from the cave vision, and their inside contacts.

14. **They try to stop it.** The gang throws everything at preventing the annihilation. Sabotage. Intervention. ArcSec contacts. Every strategy, every advantage, deployed. Not enough. Cole has been building this for centuries. Every contingency they imagine, he imagined lifetimes ago.

15. **The oil gate manifests.** Cole uses ArcSec's full infrastructure to open the gate between realms. Massive, jet-black curtains dripping Nacreid blood into turbulent ocean. The boundary between the Nacreid realm and Parhelia tears open.

16. **Cole triggers the flood.** Nacreids pour into Parhelia. The forgotten rule activates. Both races annihilating each other.

### Part III: The Oil Gate and the Final Fight

17. **The ultimate decision.** Both races are destroying each other. The annihilation cannot be stopped. Cole is on the other side of the oil gate.

18. **Flint walks through the Oil Gate.** Flint walks through alone. Cam, Liv, Milton, and Iselle remain behind. He walks toward his twin brother knowing what happens on the other side could end both of them.

19. **Both races gone.** Mutual annihilation complete. Nacreids and Irisyne, both races, both civilizations, the cosmic structure that created the curse, the reincarnation cycle, the windows, gone. Four survive: Cole, Flint, Iselle, and the mother (still trapped).

20. **Flint vs. Cole.** Brother against brother. Every prior encounter echoes: the kidnapping, the island fire, the carry-out. Their entire history across lifetimes.

21. **Cole's temptation.** Cole tells Flint to let Iselle die. If she dies, both brothers survive with full cosmic power. No curse. No cycle. No limits.

22. **Flint chooses mortality.** Cole offers godhood. Flint chooses love. Mortality with Iselle over cosmic power with his brother.

23. **Cole's Final Window.** Cole creates his first window without ArcSec tech. It materializes right there with him. In this act of selflessness he found out how to do it. A way for Flint and Iselle to leave. Where they are (corrupted water realm, Parhelia, or somewhere else) needs figuring out.

24. **The curse breaks.** Cole's annihilation of both races broke the curse. The cosmic structure that made Flint and Iselle's union dangerous no longer exists.

25. **Cole dies.** The mother's fate is unresolved: does she die with Cole? Does she stay alive?

### Part IV: Resolution

26. **Simply human.** No cosmic essence. No reincarnation. No curse. No windows. No war. Two people on Earth who love each other. Just people.

27. **Cam, Liv, and Milton.** They made it. Cam explains how they dispatched the guards during the heist, comedic relief after the intensity of Cole's death. The found family is intact.

**Book 5 ends.**
