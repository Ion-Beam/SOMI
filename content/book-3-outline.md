# BOOK 3: THE WATER REALM

**POV:** Flint (primary), Cam & Liv (parallel thread)
**Era:** Time-frozen for Flint; real-world time advances for Cam & Liv

---

## Thread A: Flint (Water Realm)

1. **Flint wakes in the water realm.** Seven suns in a nacreous sky. An inch of still water in every direction, flat to the horizon. Flint is alone. His last memory is the club: the flash behind Iselle, what came out of her mouth, the window closing.

2. **The first window.** Flint spots a speck on the horizon and runs toward it for what would have been a full day. It's a window standing alone in the water, no wall around it, old yellowed curtains tattered and moving in a wind that isn't there. He looks through the glass and sees the burned island: rain, stripped trees, charcoal ground. He watches himself arrive too late, slipping across charred ground, shouting, hitting the ground on both knees. He presses his hands against the glass. He can't go through. He can only watch.

3. **Days of walking.** Flint walks for days alone. No landmarks, no night, no change. He processes Cole, the club, Iselle, the first forest window, what he chose and what it cost. Everything that defined him (brute force, charging forward) is useless here. There's nothing to fight, nothing to climb. The space doesn't resist him. It absorbs everything. His identity begins to crack.

4. **Milton arrives.** Flint spots something in the distance, something small in the water. As he gets closer he sees it moving, swimming. Milton. Flint yells out to him and starts running. Milton starts swimming toward Flint. The ground drops without warning into deep water. Flint goes under, not expecting it. He swims toward Milton. For a moment he loses his bearings, can't tell where the shallow was, but he uses the position of the seven suns to reorient and scrambles back to the shallow part with Milton on his shoulder. No explanation for how Milton got here. He's just there.

5. **The water realm with Milton.** After finding Milton, things change. The realm opens up: more windows appear, more locations, more to see. Flint is no longer alone and the space seems to respond to that. Milton leads Flint to places and thresholds Flint would have walked right past on his own.

6. **The Cave Vision.** Flint enters a cave-like space within the water realm. An iguana-mask figure appears. The figure delivers the mythic origin story:

   - Two races existed before humans. The Nacreids, beings of desire, impulse, and destructive force, lived eternal lives. The Irisyne, beings of empathy, logic, and structure, lived finite lives and kept diligent records of each one so they could pick up where they left off when reborn.
   - The prophecy. Flint's mother was a Nacreid. She fell in love with the Irisyne King. A prophecy emerged: one son, born to unlock his true rage potential and open a portal to Parhelia for the Nacreids to flood through and destroy the Irisyne, finally settling the war between the two races.
   - The prophecy said one son. There were two, twins. Flint has a twin brother.
   - Flint was isolated, alone, different in a way that set him apart from the other Nacreids. Iselle watched him from the sky, from Parhelia. She saw he was different. Her curiosity became fascination, and fascination became love. She descended to meet him.
   - The Nacreids saw her coming down and sent Nacreid warriors to capture her, flying past Flint in a molten formation only warriors know how to achieve. He panicked and ran toward where she was going to land. When he arrived, the warriors were standing over a cowering Iselle about to attack. Flint lost it for the first time and tore them apart in front of her.
   - When they were gone, his attention turned toward Iselle, who was even more afraid now. He reached to touch her face but it burned her. In that moment the Irisyne King had made it down with his army. They captured Flint and took him up to Parhelia.
   - Flint remained captured in Parhelia awaiting trial. He was found guilty. The unjust verdict after all his time in captivity triggered an unbound rage that created a gate from the Nacreid realm into Parhelia. Nacreids flooded through and began destroying everything.
   - Iselle saw the destruction from her tower. She understood she was the only one who could stop it. She came down, approached Flint, placed a hand on him, and kissed him. But something went wrong. Instead of stopping the destruction, their union triggered an annihilation, an extinction event the prophecy never intended. A curse was born that wasn't part of the prophecy, binding Flint and Iselle across lifetimes.
   - The Nacreids were forced into the earth. The Irisyne left to become the stars. The event created humans, a new race flawed by the mixing of Nacreid and Irisyne traits.
   - Flint and Iselle were cursed to reincarnate for eternity, fall in love, but never be able to be together, meeting some form of tragic ending every time.
   - Flint sees himself as what he truly is: a pawn, a bomb, a tool used to bring down the enemy, cursed to fall in love and face the tragedy of never being able to be together for eternity.

7. **Flint's Willed Surrender.** During or immediately after the cave vision, Flint makes a choice. He surrenders everything: the ego, the brute-force identity, the way he's moved through the world across two books. He accepts who he is and what he is. He's presented with a choice: give in to destruction, or try to defeat the curse and make things right. He chooses to make things right.

## Thread B: Cam & Liv (Real World / ArcSec)

1. **ArcSec detains Cam and Liv after the club heist.** The heist attracted ArcSec's attention. Cam and Liv are separated and interrogated. They're inside the machine, the organization that monitors windows, that killed Iselle.

2. **They see ArcSec from the inside.** The scale is staggering: advanced technology, institutional culture, and a level of organization that makes the found family's window experiments look like children playing. They learn that ArcSec uses volunteers of Nacreid and Irisyne descent, fusing them to create their own windows. They're prisoners in a world they didn't know existed.

3. **They make connections inside ArcSec.** During detention, Cam and Liv encounter one or a few people who treat them as human beings rather than assets. These connections become inside contacts, fragile, formed under duress, built on thin trust.

4. **A dark figure arrives at ArcSec.** A young, muscular guy with ice blue eyes, dressed impeccably. Cam and Liv observe his arrival. They notice something off about him but can't articulate what. He's just another recruit.

5. **Cam and Liv are released after roughly 1 to 3 months.** Back in the real world, the life they had before (the DJ life, the found family, the club scene) is gone. Flint is missing. Iselle is dead. Milton is missing.

6. **They watch the recruit's rise through ArcSec over years.** The guy with the ice blue eyes rises through ArcSec's ranks at an alarming pace. His influence reshapes the organization and how it affects the real world. The changes unfold gradually but unmistakably.

7. **Their inside contacts become a lifeline.** The connections from detention evolve into an information pipeline: intelligence about what's happening inside ArcSec as the recruit rises, his movements, his decisions, the institutional changes he's driving.

8. **The inside contact provides ArcSec technology.** At a dire moment, their contact provides technology that can locate a place they've heard about called a frozen beach, where even the waves are frozen in time. Getting this tech is a risk for everyone. The technology is unstable.

9. **Cam and Liv commit fully to finding Flint.** Everything else falls away: their music, their safety, their place in the world. They commit to the frozen beach.

---

## Convergence: The Frozen Beach

1. **The frozen beach.** ArcSec technology brought Cam and Liv here. The beach is frozen, the waves themselves are frozen in place. Ice, stillness, the physical world locked mid-motion.

2. **Flint and Milton exit the water realm.** At the frozen beach, Flint and Milton step back into the real world. Flint is the same age he was when he went in; the water realm froze time for him. Internally, he's a completely different person.

3. **The reunion.** Flint sees Cam and Liv for the first time since the club heist. He went in as a broken, reckless kid. He comes out complete, someone who understands who he is, what he is, and what Iselle means. Cam and Liv sacrificed years, their safety, and their old life to find him. The found family reconstitutes, forged by what each side went through separately.

4. **Cam and Liv deliver the news.** They describe the recruit they watched rise through ArcSec: young, muscular, ice blue eyes, dressed impeccably. Flint tells them who it is. Cole. He has risen fast in ArcSec. He has plans to execute something big. But the specifics of what he's built are not yet clear. Cam and Liv know something is coming but not the full scope.

**Book 3 ends.** Flint's transformation combined with Cam and Liv's intelligence gives them the full picture. Neither side had it alone. Together, they know what they need to do: find Iselle. That's the priority. Cole and ArcSec come after. Something from the water realm, whether it's the cave vision, Milton, or the realm itself, has left Flint certain Iselle is still alive.
