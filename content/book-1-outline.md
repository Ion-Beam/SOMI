# BOOK 1: FLINT'S WORLD

**POV:** Flint (primary), Iselle intercuts
**Era:** TBD (somewhere in the 1930s to 1990s range, possibly 90s)

---

## Sequence of Events

1. **Flint's life before windows.** Flint is a teenager whose world feels too small. He spends his time at the climbing wall and at school. Cole is dating Iselle, who Flint has complicated feelings about. He admires her directness, her intelligence, and keeps whatever else he feels buried beneath the surface. Cole holds social space effortlessly and has regular access to Iselle's life in a way Flint doesn't. Flint's friends are Theo Park, who follows ideas to their furthest conclusions, and Mira Santos, who is close with Iselle and perceptive about people. Flint skateboards to school, sneaks in through the loading dock, draws figures in his notebook margins: wispy beings, dark stone creatures, an iguana in a robe he's been sketching for years, a tuxedo cat on its shoulder.

2. **Iselle's POV intercuts.** Iselle takes the ArcSec Aptitude Test, a recurring assessment she's been called to every cycle since freshman year. The questions are strange and probing: what would you do with a door that appeared on your wall? How do you explain recognizing someone you've never met? She answers with precision and without hesitation. The proctor writes more than her answers warrant. He tells her "some doors only open once" and leaves. She writes one line in her notebook and goes back to class. Cole takes the aptitude test on the same day. In a separate intercut ("Loose Hold"), Iselle returns to the climbing gym on a day Flint doesn't show up. She notices the jug hold on the overhang route is loose and spins it. Cole arrives at the gym while she's remembering Flint falling.

3. **The Forest Window.** Flint is jumped on his way home from school. Three guys in ski masks, one with shoes Flint recognizes. They bag his head, throw him in a trunk, and drive him to a forest. When they release him, they're armed: a crossbow, a machete, a rifle. They give him a five-second head start and hunt him. Flint runs. An arrow embeds in a tree next to his ear. A shot cracks past him. He reaches a cliff with nowhere to go. The guy with the machete is closing in. Flint jumps. He falls through undergrowth and lands hard in the forest. A tear in reality, a window, opens. Flint goes through it.

4. **The club and the found family.** The window deposits Flint somewhere impossible, and he ends up at a club. There he meets Cameron (Cam), Olivia (Liv), and Milton, a cat. Cam and Liv are a couple, DJs, musicians, deeply bonded to each other. The four of them form a found family.

5. **The van ride.** Cam, Liv, Milton, and Flint ride home together. Flint gets to know them: who they are, what they do, how they operate. They form a bond through the strangeness of this experience.

6. **Flint tells Iselle.** The next day, Flint tells Iselle about the forest and the window. They go back to the forest together. The window is gone, nothing there but trees. But Iselle believes him. She doesn't need proof. Flint connects with Cam and Liv, cementing the found family's communication line.

7. **Window experimentation.** Flint tries to make windows happen again. Duds, failed attempts, nothing. Brute force doesn't work. He can't will a window into existence by pushing hard enough or wanting it badly enough. The power has rules he doesn't understand.

8. **Cole escalates.** Cole's possessiveness over Iselle intensifies: surveillance, controlling behavior, showing up where he shouldn't be.

9. **The real window.** Flint finally triggers a genuine window, and the key is emotion, not force. When he's near what he truly wants, the window responds. He opens a window to a fjord, stunning, impossible, real. Iselle develops a theory: the windows respond to proximity to what you truly want. Genuine desire, not brute force, is the mechanism.

10. **The fracture.** Cole and Iselle break up. The social world around all three of them cracks. The triangle is destabilized.

11. **The Prom Kiss.** At prom, Flint and Iselle kiss. Every light in the gym blows out simultaneously. Cole is watching from somewhere in the room.

12. **Wild Goose Island.** It's tradition after prom for everyone in the school to go to Wild Goose Island, where they have a bonfire and party. Cole forces a kiss on Iselle. Flint's rage is immediate and physical. He fights Cole on the island. A fire starts. Flint pulls his punches. Cole wins the fight. Flint blacks out.

13. **Rain and retreat.** Flint wakes up on a random shore in time to see the entire island up in flames. He doesn't know how he got out of there. It rains. Iselle pulls away from Flint, not rejecting him, but afraid of what Cole will do next.

14. **Flint with the found family.** Flint spends the weekend with Cam, Liv, and Milton. He hasn't heard from Iselle. Who Cam and Liv are beyond the DJ set: their music, their bond, their humor. Milton's quiet presence. The relationships that will carry the series forward are established here, with Flint inside them rather than observing from the outside.

15. **The Ash Window.** Iselle misses school. She's never missed school. Flint, while with Cam, Liv, and Milton after school, comes to the realization that she may have gone back to the island. Something lines up that points to her going that day (needs figuring out). He runs to the lake in the rain, gets a canoe off the rack, and paddles out alone. The crossing is rough, wind broadside, waves over the bow. He grounds on the rocky shore, drags the canoe onto a boulder, and pushes inland. The island is black: stripped trees, charred trunks, wet charcoal ground. Through the gaps between burned trees, he sees a figure standing still, and beside her, a window. Ash-gray curtains hanging motionless between two burned trunks despite the wind. Flint runs, shouting her name. Iselle is standing at the window with her back to him. She steps through. Once she's on the other side, she turns around and closes the window. The window is gone. Flint hits the ground where it was on both knees, pushing his hands through empty air.

**Book 1 ends.** Iselle is gone. Flint's world has been narrowed to a single objective: find her.
