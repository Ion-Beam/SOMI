# BOOK 4: ISELLE'S BOOK

**POV:** Iselle
**Era:** Modern or near-future (however many lifetimes after Book 1)

---

## Sequence of Events

1. **The Victorian dream.** A Victorian-era Iselle grieves over a dead Flint, his body, the loss. Then Iselle wakes up. It was a dream, one of the visions she gets in her sleep, flashes of lives that arrive uninvited. She's in her current life, modern era, and the grief fades into the strangeness of something remembered from a life she didn't live.

2. **Iselle in her current life.** Iselle is competent, independent, and navigates a world she understands better than anyone around her realizes. She has the Book of Bridges, a physical notebook (or collection of notebooks) accumulated across every lifetime she's lived. Entries in the same handwriting, different languages, different eras. This is the Irisyne way: they live finite lives, retain no memories, but keep a diligent record of each one so the next version can pick up where the last left off. It is Iselle's nature to find and follow this record. Everything she knows, she knows because a previous version of herself wrote it down.

3. **She found him.** It took a long time, longer than any entry in the notebook describes, but she found someone matching what the entries describe. She's with him now. He's familiar in the ways that matter. The notebook's pattern holds.

4. **Past lives through the notebook.** Iselle reads entries from the Book of Bridges and the story shifts into those past lifetimes: the love, the danger, the ending. Then back to Iselle in her current life. Centuries of falling in love with the same person and losing him every time.

5. **Dreams as exceptions.** Flashes of past lives come to Iselle in her sleep: the Victorian grief from the opening, possibly other moments. Visceral, emotional, uncontrolled. They arrive without context and fade fast. The notebook is the systematic channel. The dreams are something older and less controllable.

6. **The relationship.** She's with him. Things are good. But something she can't name doesn't sit right, tiny, not enough to act on or even call doubt. Meanwhile, ArcSec is changing in ways she notices through the notebook's records of the organization across eras. Something is different about ArcSec now, but she doesn't know what.

7. **The subtle thing.** He does something small, something the person described in the entries would never have done. On its own it means nothing. But it sits in her. She goes back through the notebook. Entries from past lives where she tried to be with other men, some of them shared qualities with each other that she never connected before. A second pattern she never noticed, spread across centuries of the same handwriting. The pieces come together: he's not the person the notebook describes. He's something else. And the ArcSec unease converges at the same time. Everything shatters.

8. **The decision.** Iselle loses all hope. The person she thought she'd found isn't him. The notebook didn't fail; she misread it. She chooses to act impulsively. She's going out alone, to have a good time, because that's what she feels like doing. No plan. No system. Just going. She sits at her vanity.

9. **Her armor.** The vanity table becomes an operating table. Every product in sequence: foundation, liner, shadow, lip. The dress. The mask.

10. **The mansion party.** Iselle leaves the isolation of her room and walks into the chaos of her own mansion, where a party she's hosting is already underway. She descends the staircase. Everyone stares. She crosses the room without stopping, without engaging. Walks straight through and out the front door.

11. **The limo.** Champagne from the bottle. Standing through the sunroof, screaming into the wind. Her face buckling for a moment, the control slipping. She catches it. Breathes through it. Holds.

12. **The club.** Bass in her sternum. She dances. The control that defined her, the planner, the expert, loosens. The music puts her in her body instead of her head.

13. **The music shifts.** The tempo drops. Something changes in the room.

14. **She sees Flint.** He's there. The real one. She knows it immediately, not from the notebook, not from the entries. From something deeper. Everything the person she was with wasn't, this person is.

15. **The vision.** Iselle gives in completely. Everything comes flooding back, not from the notebook, not from the entries. From somewhere deeper. The notebook says choosing love ends the same way every time. Previous entries document her pulling back at this exact moment, going back to the plan, trusting the system. Not this time. She chooses love anyway.

16. **She snaps out of it.** The real Flint is looking at her. He recognizes her immediately.

17. **The moment.** Flint crosses the room to her. They have the moment, giving themselves to each other completely.

18. **The interruption.** They are about to kiss, but they need to go through the club window again. The window from Iselle's lifetime was shut and the frozen beach window closed due to its instability from being opened with ArcSec tech, something they haven't fully mastered. The club's control window is the only way back into the water realm. The heist replays, same club, same approach. But this time they do it together: Flint, Iselle, Cam, Liv, Milton. The whole found family, reunited.

19. **The heist replays.** At first it follows the same steps from Book 2, same fog, same approach. But at the last moment, things change. Cam and Liv are barely seen right before Flint and Iselle go through the window. It looks like Cam, Liv, and possibly Milton have given their lives. This is ambiguous.

20. **The White Mist Union.** Flint and Iselle go through the window together. They enter the white mist. They choose each other in love, in something that resembles a marriage. Cam, Liv, and Milton are there with them.

21. **Cole.** The full picture comes together. Iselle learns that Cole is the one who has corrupted ArcSec. He has a plan to destroy everything. The man she was with, the man she thought was Flint, is the architect of all of it. How she finds this out needs figuring out.

**Book 4 ends.** Iselle chose love. Flint chose love. The found family is whole. And Cole has been building toward annihilation.
